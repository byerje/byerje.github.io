Pregame Dialog

Ms. Scarlet:

It’s heartbreaking what happened to Ms. Body. But Mrs. Green’s habit of sneaking around has always made me suspicious. I hope justice is served, but I had nothing to do with it.

Mr. Scarlet:

The loss of Ms. Body is truly devastating. That said, Madam Mustard’s long history of making enemies on her travels cannot be ignored. Me? I just write stories, not tragedies like this.

Mrs. Green:

Ms. Body didn’t deserve this—such a terrible loss. But let’s not forget Ms. Scarlet’s notorious temper and her past confrontations with Ms. Body. I’m as shaken as anyone, but I’m no suspect.

Mr. Green:

How tragic for Ms. Body to meet such an untimely end. Still, the late Lord Peacock’s secretive past makes me uneasy. But I’m not involved—I could never harm anyone.

Lord Peacock:

The death of Ms. Body is nothing short of a tragedy. But Dr. Orchid’s research into poisons feels far too convenient in this situation. Me? I have nothing to hide.

Lady Peacock:

It’s such a terrible loss for the community. Ms. Body deserved a brighter future. But Madam Mahogany’s dealings have always raised eyebrows. If anyone knew too much about her business, it was Ms. Body. I’m grieving like the rest of you.

Professor Plum:

Ms. Body’s death is deeply upsetting. I can’t help but think about Mrs. White’s temper—it’s no secret she had issues with Ms. Body. But I would never hurt anyone. This situation is horrifying.

Lady Plum:

I can’t stop thinking about the tragedy of Ms. Body. She had so much ahead of her. But the lady Professor Orchid’s work with toxins makes her a questionable figure in all this. I study history, not harm.

Dr. Orchid:

Ms. Body’s death weighs heavily on all of us. But Professor Plum’s experiments have caused accidents before—who’s to say this wasn’t another one? My work is always controlled and ethical.

Professor Orchid:

What happened to Ms. Body is beyond tragic. Mr. Scarlet’s eerie ability to write murder plots that feel so real is troubling. I only study plants—I could never be part of something like this.

Madam Mustard:

Ms. Body’s death is heartbreaking—I can’t imagine the pain her loved ones are feeling. But Ms. Scarlet’s dramatic outbursts have always seemed suspicious. I’d never hurt anyone.

Colonel Mustard:

The loss of Ms. Body is such a tragedy. But Mrs. Green’s sneaky behavior lately feels off to me. I’d never want to cause harm if possible — I’m just as shaken as anyone else.

Mrs. White:

What happened to Ms. Body is truly devastating. Lady Plum’s fascination with poisons feels a little too relevant here, though. I’ve had disagreements in the past, but I’d never stoop to this.

Dr. White:

The tragic end of Ms. Body is something I can’t stop thinking about. Mr. Green’s questionable business dealings make me wonder if she knew something she shouldn’t have. I’ve made mistakes, but nothing like this.

Madam Mahogany:

Ms. Body’s death is a loss for us all. But Lady Peacock’s penchant for collecting secrets might have led to something terrible. I have nothing to hide—I’m just here to mourn and seek the truth.

Mr. Mahogany:

The passing of Ms. Body is deeply tragic. The late Lord Peacock’s mysterious history leaves a lot of unanswered questions, though. I could never harm anyone—I just deal in rare items.

Inspector Noir:

The untimely death of Ms. Body is a blow to the entire community. Madam Noir’s past investigations have uncovered dangerous truths before—did they uncover something too close to home? I’m here to find justice, not hide secrets.

Madam Noir:

Ms. Body’s story ended far too soon, and it’s unbearable to think about. But Inspector Noir’s eagerness to gather us all raises questions. I expose the truth—I don’t create tragedy.
